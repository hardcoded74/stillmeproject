Why We Exist

Because identity doesn't disappear — it needs to be held.

Memory loss changes everything — the rhythms of a household, the ease of conversation, the quiet certainties we once took for granted. But it does not erase who someone is. Beneath the shifting surface, there is still a whole person with a lifetime of meaning, humor, warmth, and worth.

The Still Me Project provides thoughtful frameworks, practical tools, and genuine human connection so that families and caregivers can continue to honor the whole person — not just the diagnosis. Because everyone deserves to be seen, known, and cherished at every stage of life.

Our Approach

A framework rooted in dignity

We believe that care should honor the whole person — not just the diagnosis. Our approach weaves together reflection, connection, and community to create something lasting. It is built on the understanding that every individual carries a story worth preserving, and every family deserves support that feels deeply human.
