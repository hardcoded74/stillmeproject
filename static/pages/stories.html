A Story Worth Sharing

Finding Her Voice Again

When Margaret’s family first came to us, they felt like they were losing her a little more each day. She had stopped speaking much, and the silence was heavy. But through gentle, patient work — music she once loved, photographs from her kitchen table, her daughter’s steady voice reading old letters — something shifted. Margaret began to hum. Then she began to sing. And then, one afternoon, she looked at her daughter and said her name.

This is the kind of moment we live for. Not a cure, but a connection. Not a fix, but a return to something true.
