About the Still Me Project

Who we are and why we do this work.

Our Story

Where it began

The Still Me Project was born from a deeply personal place — a belief that memory loss should never mean the loss of identity. When someone you love begins to forget, it can feel like the world is asking you to let go too. We started this work because we refused to accept that. We believed that with the right tools, the right support, and the right kind of attention, the essence of a person could be held, honored, and carried forward.

What began as quiet conversations between families and caregivers has grown into something larger — a community of people who understand that dignity is not contingent on memory. We have seen what happens when someone is truly seen: the way a familiar song can light up a room, the way a daughter’s voice can bring a moment of peace, the way a well-told story can bridge the distance that memory loss creates.

Today, the Still Me Project works with families, caregivers, and communities to build frameworks that preserve identity and strengthen connection. We create resources that are warm, practical, and deeply human — because that is what this work demands. Every person we serve reminds us why this matters. Every story we help hold is a testament to the enduring power of love.

Our Values

What We Believe

♥

Every person has a story worth holding

No matter how memory shifts, the life someone has lived carries meaning. Their joys, their struggles, their humor, their love — these things do not vanish. They deserve to be gathered, cherished, and shared.

•

Connection is the foundation of care

The most powerful thing we can offer someone navigating memory loss is the assurance that they are not alone. True care lives in relationship — in the people who show up, listen, and stay present.

♦

Dignity is not a diagnosis

A medical diagnosis does not define a person’s worth. Dignity belongs to every individual at every stage of life, and it is our responsibility to protect it with intention, compassion, and unwavering respect.
